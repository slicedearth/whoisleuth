1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains what information WHOISleuth (the "Service") processes when you use it, why, and what choices you have. It applies to this deployment, operated by the maintainer of the WHOISleuth GitHub repository ("we", "us"). By using the Service, you agree to the practices described here.

2. Information We Collect

Personal data. Looking up a domain returns whatever registrant contact data (name, organisation, email, phone, address) that domain's registry chooses to publish in its RDAP/WHOIS response. We don't collect this ourselves - it's already-public registry data we're relaying at your request. Most registries redact it by default, so many lookups return none at all.

Technical data. Standard web request metadata (IP address, timestamps) is processed transiently to enforce login and rate limits (see Section 8) and is not stored beyond that.

Cookies. See Section 6.

We do not run analytics, advertising, or third-party tracking scripts of any kind.

3. How We Use Your Data

  • To perform the lookup, scan, or search you explicitly requested.
  • To keep you signed in (the session cookie described in Section 6).
  • To enforce rate limits that protect the Service and upstream registries from abuse.

Registrant data surfaced in a lookup is never used by us for any purpose beyond displaying it to you - we don't build profiles, sell it, or share it with advertisers.

4. Legal Basis for Processing

Where GDPR or similar law applies, we rely on legitimate interest for running lookups and anti-abuse controls you've directly requested, and on your own consent/decision when you choose to export, save, or act on data the Service shows you (e.g. drafting outreach or an abuse report to a registrant). Using the outreach feature to contact a registrant is closer to direct marketing than a lookup is - keep it low-volume, human-reviewed (the Service already requires manually sending each draft), and honor any request to stop being contacted.

5. Data Sharing & Third Parties

We do not sell or rent personal data. Data may pass through:

  • The domain's own registry/registrar and crt.sh - the sources a lookup queries live, on demand, per request. They're the origin of the data, not a service we hand your data to.
  • Hosting infrastructure - if this deployment runs on Netlify, request handling occurs on Netlify's infrastructure; if self-hosted, on whatever server the operator runs it on.

We don't use any other sub-processors (no analytics, ad, or CRM vendors).

6. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

The Service sets exactly one cookie: a signed session token (wrt_session) that keeps you logged in for up to 30 days. It's HttpOnly, SameSite=Lax, and Secure where served over HTTPS. This cookie is strictly necessary for the login you explicitly requested - it is not used for tracking, analytics, or advertising, and under most cookie-consent rules (e.g. EU/UK ePrivacy) that means it doesn't require a consent banner.

The Shortlist and Watchlist features use your browser's localStorage - saved only on your device, never transmitted to us. Clearing your browser's site data removes it.

7. Data Retention

Lookup results are not retained server-side at all - each request is handled and discarded. Shortlist/Watchlist data persists in your browser's local storage until you remove it yourself (each panel has a "Clear all" option) or clear your browser's site data. If you export a CSV/JSON file, that file is yours to manage from that point on - delete it once you no longer need it.

8. Data Security

Technical measures in place include a shared-password session gate with signed, HttpOnly cookies; per-IP rate limiting on login and lookup routes; and SSRF/DNS-rebinding-guarded outbound requests when checking a domain's own homepage. No system is perfectly secure, and the Service is provided as is, with no warranty (see LICENSE).

9. Your Rights

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or restrict processing of your personal data, and to data portability or objection. Since the Service keeps no server-side database, exercising a request against data you've *shared with us* (mainly: nothing, given we don't store anything server-side) or against data *we've displayed to you* is best directed to the domain's own registry/registrar, who is the actual data controller for that registrant record. For anything you've personally exported or saved (Shortlist/Watchlist/CSV/JSON), you're in direct control already - see Section 7.

To reach us about this Service specifically, open an issue on the GitHub repository.

10. International Data Transfers

Because lookups query the relevant registry directly (which may be located anywhere in the world) and hosting infrastructure (e.g. Netlify) may process requests across regions, data may be transferred internationally as an inherent part of how RDAP/WHOIS and web hosting work. We do not otherwise transfer data to additional third parties.

11. Children's Privacy

The Service is not directed at children and is not knowingly used to collect personal data from children.
